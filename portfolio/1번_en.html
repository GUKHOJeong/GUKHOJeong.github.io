2. Challenge (Situation & Task)

üßê Situation

Instability in power supply is increasing due to climate change. Accurate prediction of individual household power consumption can induce energy saving and contribute to national power supply stability.

üöß Task

The goal was to build a model that accurately predicts future (2024) data without overfitting, by capturing the nonlinearity of power consumption patterns based on various climate data (2016~2024) such as temperature, humidity, and precipitation.

3. Process & Action

üìä Data Preprocessing & EDA

  • Data Merge: Integration of KEPCO (Power), KMA (Weather), and KOGAS data based on time series.
  • Correlation Analysis: Verified correlation coefficients between temperature/humidity and power consumption via Heatmap (Positive correlation identified).

üîç Feature Engineering Strategy

Five experimental groups were designed to solve high correlation (multicollinearity) between climate variables and prevent overfitting.

  • X0 (Baseline): Using all variables for baseline performance measurement.
  • X2 (PCA): "Too many variables" -> Dimensionality reduction to minimize information loss while reducing computational cost.
  • X3 (VIF): "Removing redundancy" -> Removed variables with Variance Inflation Factor (VIF) over 10 to ensure regression reliability.
  • X4 (Stepwise): Selected only statistically significant variables to improve model interpretability.

We experimented with 8 different algorithms to find the optimal prediction performance.

4. Result

When validated against 2022 data which showed typical climate patterns, the XGBoost model closely followed actual power usage patterns and achieved high prediction accuracy (R¬≤: 0.92), proving its ability to learn time-series patterns.

üìâ Phase 2: Impact of Climate Crisis

[Train: ~2023 | Test: 2024 (Heatwave)]

  • Situation: Trained up to 2023 and predicted 2024.
  • Result: While the model followed the overall Monthly Trend, it under-predicted the surge in power usage during peak summer months (July-Sept).

5. Retrospective & Limitations

üìâ Cause of Error: "2024 Record Heatwave & Tropical Nights"

Analysis of the performance drop in Phase 2 (2024) confirmed that it was due to unprecedented climate patterns (Outliers) outside the training data range (2016~2023). (Source: KMA 2024 Summer Climate Analysis)

  • 1. Record High Temperatures (Extreme Heat):
    The average summer temperature in 2024 was 25.6‚ÑÉ, 1.9‚ÑÉ higher than normal (23.7‚ÑÉ), ranking 1st since observations began in 1973. It was an 'Outlier' hard to predict with past data.
  • 2. Continuous Tropical Nights:
    The average number of tropical nights was 20.3 days, 3.1 times more than normal (6.5 days). This persistent nighttime heat caused a spike in AC usage, significantly increasing total monthly power consumption beyond model predictions.

üí° Insight & Action: I realized the limitation that learning solely from past data is insufficient to catch up with rapid pattern changes (Drift) due to the 'Climate Crisis'. I identified the need to introduce an 'Online Learning' pipeline that reflects real-time weather forecasts or assigns weights to outliers in the future.
