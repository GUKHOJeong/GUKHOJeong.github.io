3. A Key Achievement

In a project to analyze changes in marine fishing catch distribution based on climate change, we focused on understanding and predicting the impact of marine climate factors on fishing yields. However, due to the extreme volatility and complexity of the catch data, we faced challenges in ensuring predictive accuracy, with high RMSE and MAE scores.

Despite these quantitative limitations, our team persevered, delving deeply into domestic and international research papers and case studies. This process led us to a critical insight: contrary to our initial assumptions, the primary drivers of catch fluctuations were not changes in water temperature and the marine environment, but rather direct human fishing activities and policy intervention.

This realization was a crucial turning point. It moved us beyond simply improving a predictive model and allowed us to propose practical policy directions for managing fishing yields and conserving marine resources. Through this experience, I gained a broader perspective on understanding the inherent limitations of data and grasping the essence of complex problems. Ultimately, this project equipped me with the data-driven decision-making skills needed to identify core issues and drive tangible change.
